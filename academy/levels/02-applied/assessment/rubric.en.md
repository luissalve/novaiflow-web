# Level 2 · Grading Rubric

This rubric explains exactly how your Level 2 work is evaluated for the certificate. The goal is simple
and human: confirm your team can build a few **reusable, tested, responsible templates** and gather
them into a shared kit. There is no coding, no trick exam — your real deliverable is the heart of it.

For the full program-wide policy (pass criteria, resubmission, what the certificate attests), see
`docs/ASSESSMENT-CERTIFICATION.en.md`. This file is the Level 2 detail behind that summary.

---

## 1. What gets scored

Three parts make up your Level 2 score. The **deliverable** (your team template kit) carries the most
weight.

| Component | Weight | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Deliverable** — the team template kit | 70% | The real artifact: 5+ reusable templates gathered into one shared document, each a card with `{variables}`, a when-to-use note, and a check-before-sending line |
| **Participation** — workshop engagement | 20% | Building and testing templates during the workshop, helping your group, refining (not restarting), naming an owner |
| **Quiz** — optional knowledge check | 10% | The 10-question check in `assessment/quiz.en.md` |

> If your team chooses **not** to run the quiz, those 10 points fold into the deliverable, which then
> counts for **80%**. Your instructor confirms the scheme at the start of the session.

---

## 2. Pass threshold

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Overall score** | **≥ 70%** to earn the certificate |
| **Deliverable submitted and usable** | **Required** — pass/fail gate (5+ templates must exist, cover 3+ types, and work) |
| **Attendance** (In-company / Live online) | **≥ 80%** of session time |
| **Responsible-use gate** | A kit that bakes sensitive data (PHI/PII/confidential) into a template, or that has no review step, **does not pass**, regardless of score |

Not passing on the first try is normal — AI skills are built by doing. You get written feedback and may
**revise and resubmit once** within 14 days at no extra cost.

---

## 3. The deliverable rubric — your team template kit (70%)

The kit is judged on five dimensions. We use three plain levels: **Needs work · Good · Excellent**. To
pass, the kit should land at **Good or above** overall.

| Dimension | Needs work | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Reusability** (uses `{variables}`, locked frame) | Mostly one-off prompts; few or no variables | Variables marked; role, tone and format are locked | Cleanly templated; a teammate can run any card cold with no edits |
| **2. Coverage** (size and variety) | Fewer than 5 templates, or all one type | 5+ templates across 3+ types (email, summary, spreadsheet, image) | 5+ strong templates across all four types, mapped to real recurring tasks |
| **3. Tested & reliable** | Untested, or tried once | Each tested on two real (non-sensitive) cases | Tested, and the card notes what to watch for so results stay consistent |
| **4. Clarity of cards** (name, when-to-use, owner) | Cards missing fields; hard to follow | All fields present; a teammate could find and use it | Crisp, searchable, organized; the kit reads like a real team standard |
| **5. Responsible use** | Sensitive data inside, or no review step | No sensitive data; every card has a check-before-sending line | No sensitive data; clear review step **and** verification notes on facts/figures |

### How the three levels map to a score

These levels align with the program's scoring bands so your certificate is consistent across levels.

| Level (this rubric) | Band (program) | Score | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Excellent** | Exceeds | 90–100% | Beyond the bar; a kit another team could adopt as-is |
| **Good** | Meets | 70–89% | Solid, usable kit — **passing** |
| **Needs work** | Developing | 50–69% | On the way; revise and resubmit |
| (below) | Not yet | < 50% | Another attempt with support |

**Scoring the kit:** the instructor scores each of the five dimensions and weights the result as the
70% deliverable component. A single weak template will not sink the kit if the rest are strong — but the
kit must hit the minimum size (5+ templates, 3+ types) and every card must have a check-before-sending
line.

---

## 4. Participation rubric (20%)

Participation is about engaging with the live build — not about being the loudest person in the room.

| Behavior | Needs work | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Built in the workshop** | Watched only | Helped build and test at least one template | Built several, tested each on two cases, refined instead of restarting |
| **Worked with the group** | Did not participate | Contributed to the group's templates | Helped a peer, shared a useful pattern, or took the owner role |
| **Applied the concepts** | Did not use templates/variables | Used `{variables}` and the template card | Used variables, a second-case test, **and** a responsible-use check naturally |

---

## 5. Quiz contribution (10%)

The 10-question quiz (`assessment/quiz.en.md`) contributes up to 10% and has **no minimum on its own**.
It confirms the concepts landed: one-off prompt vs. reusable template, variables, summaries and tables,
model choice, image prompts, and the responsible-use rules. If the quiz is not run, this weight folds
into the deliverable.

---

## 6. Quick self-check before you submit

Run your kit through this list. If you can tick every box, you are ready.

- [ ] The kit has **5+ templates** across **3+ types** (email, summary, spreadsheet, image).
- [ ] Every template uses **`{variables}`** for the parts that change.
- [ ] Every template was **tested on two** real, non-sensitive cases.
- [ ] Every card ends with a **check-before-sending** line.
- [ ] A teammate used **one card with no extra help**.
- [ ] **No sensitive data** anywhere in the kit.
- [ ] The kit has a **named owner**.
