# Exercise 4 — Write an On-Brand Image Brief

AI can generate images — a social graphic, a simple banner, a product mockup, an illustration for a
flyer. But a vague request ("make a nice image for our promo") gives you something generic and
off-brand. A good **image brief** is just like a good prompt: it names the subject, the style, the
colors, the layout, and any text — clearly enough that the result fits your brand the first time.

In this exercise you'll write a reusable image-brief template for a real need and generate a draft. The
brief becomes a card in your **team template kit**.

> **Time:** about 15 minutes · **You need:** any image-generating AI tool (the one used in class) and a
> real, simple visual need. Keep it brand-safe: no faces of real people, no copyrighted logos or
> characters.

---

## The six parts of a clear image brief

| Part | What to specify | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject** | What is in the image | "a delivery truck on a coastal highway" |
| **Style** | The look and feel | "clean, modern flat illustration" |
| **Colors** | Your palette (brand red is `#E5002B`) | "white background, accents in brand red `#E5002B`" |
| **Layout** | Orientation and composition | "horizontal banner, subject on the right, open space on the left for text" |
| **Text** | Any words in the image (kept short) | the text `Fast Freight, Done Right` |
| **Mood / use** | Where it'll be used and the feeling | "for a LinkedIn post; confident and professional" |

> **Note on text in images:** AI can misspell words inside an image. Keep on-image text very short, and
> always **proofread it letter by letter** in the result.

---

## The scenario

Pick **one** simple, real visual you actually need. Examples by role:

| Your world | A simple image you could brief |
|---|---|
| Logistics / freight | A banner for a "reliable shipping" social post |
| Retail | A clean product-feature graphic for a sale |
| Real estate | A "Just Listed" social graphic (no real address/photo) |
| Hotel | A welcoming seasonal-promo banner |
| School | A friendly "Parent Night" announcement graphic |
| Construction | A simple "Now Hiring" jobsite graphic |
| Government | A clear, accessible public-notice graphic |

Keep it **generic and rights-safe**: illustrations or simple scenes, no real people's faces, no
trademarked logos, no copyrighted characters.

---

## Your task

Write a reusable **image-brief template**, fill it for your need, and generate one draft.

### Fill-in scaffold

```text
Create an image for {use, e.g. a LinkedIn post}.
Subject: {what's in it, e.g. a stylized delivery truck on a coastal highway}.
Style: {look, e.g. clean modern flat illustration, no photorealism}.
Colors: {palette}; use brand red {#E5002B} as the accent color; keep the rest {e.g. white and light gray}.
Layout: {orientation, e.g. horizontal banner}; {composition, e.g. subject on the right, open space on the left for a headline}.
Text in image: the text "{short text, e.g. Fast Freight, Done Right}" — keep it short and spelled exactly this way.
Mood: {feeling, e.g. confident, professional, friendly}.
Avoid: {e.g. real people's faces, any logos, clutter, busy backgrounds}.
```

---

## Steps

1. Choose your one simple, rights-safe visual need.
2. Fill in all six parts of the brief. Be specific about colors (use brand red `#E5002B`) and layout.
3. Generate the image.
4. Review it against the brief: right subject, style, colors, layout? Is any on-image **text spelled
   correctly**? Does it look on-brand?
5. Refine with small instructions if needed ("more white space," "make the red the accent, not the
   background," "remove the extra text").
6. Save the brief (the template, with `{variables}`) as a kit card named "Image brief — [your use]."

---

## Success looks like

- [ ] The brief specifies all six parts: **subject, style, colors, layout, text, mood/use**.
- [ ] Brand red **`#E5002B`** is named as the accent color (not a random red).
- [ ] Any text in the image is **short and spelled correctly** (you proofread it letter by letter).
- [ ] The image is **rights-safe**: no real people's faces, no copyrighted logos or characters.
- [ ] The brief is saved as a reusable template a teammate could fill in for a similar need.

> **Remember (Responsible AI):** a person reviews and **owns** every published image. Check that the
> image is accurate, that on-image text is spelled right, and that you have the **rights** to use it —
> AI can produce content that resembles protected work. Never publish without a human check.
