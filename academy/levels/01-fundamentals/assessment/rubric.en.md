# Level 1 · Grading Rubric

This rubric explains exactly how your Level 1 work is evaluated for the certificate. The goal is simple
and human: confirm you can write a few **clear, useful, responsible prompts** for your own job. There is
no coding, no trick exam — your real deliverable is the heart of it.

For the full program-wide policy (pass criteria, resubmission, what the certificate attests), see
`docs/ASSESSMENT-CERTIFICATION.en.md`. This file is the Level 1 detail behind that summary.

---

## 1. What gets scored

Three parts make up your Level 1 score. The **deliverable** (your 3 prompts) carries the most weight.

| Component | Weight | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Deliverable** — 3 role-specific prompts | 70% | The real artifact: three prompts you wrote and saved for your own work, each using Role · Task · Context · Format |
| **Participation** — workshop engagement | 20% | Practicing during the workshop, asking questions, refining (not restarting), completing the in-class exercise |
| **Quiz** — optional knowledge check | 10% | The 10-question check in `assessment/quiz.en.md` |

> If your team chooses **not** to run the quiz, those 10 points fold into the deliverable, which then
> counts for **80%**. Your instructor confirms the scheme at the start of the session.

---

## 2. Pass threshold

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Overall score** | **≥ 70%** to earn the certificate |
| **Deliverable submitted and usable** | **Required** — pass/fail gate (3 prompts must exist and work) |
| **Attendance** (In-company / Live online) | **≥ 80%** of session time |
| **Responsible-use gate** | A deliverable that pastes sensitive data (PHI/PII/confidential) into a public tool without an approved policy **does not pass**, regardless of score |

Not passing on the first try is normal — AI skills are built by doing. You get written feedback and may
**revise and resubmit once** within 14 days at no extra cost.

---

## 3. The deliverable rubric — your 3 prompts (70%)

Each of your three prompts is judged on four dimensions. We use three plain levels:
**Needs work · Good · Excellent**. To pass, your set of prompts should land at **Good or above** overall.

| Dimension | Needs work | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Clarity & structure** (uses Role · Task · Context · Format) | Vague or one-line; missing most of the formula | All four parts present; the ask is understandable | Crisp and specific; every part adds real value; easy for a coworker to reuse |
| **2. Usefulness for your role** | Generic; not tied to a real task you do | Tied to a real, recurring task in your job | Solves a task that clearly saves you time today; you would use it tomorrow |
| **3. Output quality** (the result the prompt produces) | Result is off-target or unusable | Result is useful with light editing | Result is on-target and needs little to no editing; format is exactly what you asked for |
| **4. Responsible use** | Includes sensitive data, or shows no awareness of verifying/reviewing | Avoids sensitive data; you note that a person reviews the output | Avoids sensitive data, plans a review step, and flags any fact/figure to verify before use |

### How the three levels map to a score

These levels align with the program's scoring bands so your certificate is consistent across levels.

| Level (this rubric) | Band (program) | Score | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Excellent** | Exceeds | 90–100% | Beyond the bar; could help a coworker write theirs |
| **Good** | Meets | 70–89% | Solid, usable prompts — **passing** |
| **Needs work** | Developing | 50–69% | On the way; revise and resubmit |
| (below) | Not yet | < 50% | Another attempt with support |

**Scoring the set:** the instructor scores each of the four dimensions, averages them across your three
prompts, and weights the result as the 70% deliverable component. A single weak prompt will not sink you
if the other two are strong — but all three must exist.

---

## 4. Participation rubric (20%)

Participation is about engaging with the live practice — not about being the loudest person in the room.

| Behavior | Needs work | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Practiced in the workshop** | Watched only | Wrote and tested at least one prompt | Iterated several times, refining instead of restarting |
| **Engaged** | Did not participate | Asked or answered at least once | Helped a peer or shared a useful example |
| **Applied the concepts** | Did not use the formula | Used Role · Task · Context · Format | Used the formula *and* a responsible-use check naturally |

---

## 5. Quiz contribution (10%)

The 10-question quiz (`assessment/quiz.en.md`) contributes up to 10% and has **no minimum on its own**.
It confirms the concepts landed: what generative AI is, the prompt formula, refine-don't-restart, and
the three responsible-use rules. If the quiz is not run, this weight folds into the deliverable.

---

## 6. Quick self-check before you submit

Run your three prompts through this list. If you can tick every box, you are ready.

- [ ] Each prompt names a **Role**, a **Task**, the **Context**, and the **Format** you want.
- [ ] Each is tied to a **real task** you actually do at work.
- [ ] You **tested** each one and got a useful result (you refined instead of starting over).
- [ ] **No sensitive data** (PHI/PII/confidential) is inside any prompt.
- [ ] You can point to **who reviews** the output and **which facts** you would verify before using it.
