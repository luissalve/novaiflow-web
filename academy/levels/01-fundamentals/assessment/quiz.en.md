# Level 1 · Knowledge Check (Quiz)

A short, friendly check to confirm the key ideas from Fundamentals landed. There are **10 questions** —
a mix of multiple-choice and short-answer. It is **optional** and small (worth about 10% of your level
score); your **3 prompts** deliverable is what really earns the certificate. Take about 10 minutes. You
may answer in English or Spanish.

> No tricks here. If a question makes you pause, that is a good sign — it means you are thinking like a
> careful AI user. The **answer key** is at the bottom.

---

## Instructions

1. Answer all 10 questions. For multiple-choice, write the letter. For short-answer, one or two
   sentences is plenty.
2. Use your own words — there is no need to memorize definitions.
3. When you finish, check yourself against the answer key. Anything you miss is just a quick review.

---

## Part A — Multiple choice (1–6)

**1. What is "generative AI" in plain terms?**
- A. A search engine that finds existing web pages for you
- B. A tool that *creates* new text, images, or summaries based on what you ask
- C. A spreadsheet that calculates formulas automatically
- D. A virus scanner for your computer

**2. Which of these best describes how a chat assistant produces an answer?**
- A. It looks up the single correct answer in a fixed database
- B. It predicts likely, useful wording based on patterns it learned — so it can be wrong with confidence
- C. It calls a human expert in the background to write the reply
- D. It copies an answer word-for-word from one website

**3. The prompt formula taught in this level is:**
- A. Subject · Verb · Object · Punctuation
- B. Role · Task · Context · Format
- C. Who · What · When · Where
- D. Input · Output · Save · Send

**4. Your first answer from the AI is "almost right." What is the recommended next step?**
- A. Start over from a blank chat and rewrite the whole prompt
- B. Accept it as-is to save time
- C. Refine in the same conversation — tell it what to fix or change ("refine, don't restart")
- D. Switch to a different tool entirely

**5. A clinic employee wants to summarize a patient note with a public AI chat tool. The safest move is:**
- A. Paste the full note, including the patient's name and ID, to get the best summary
- B. Remove or replace the patient's personal/health details (PHI) — or use an approved tool/policy first
- C. Paste it but ask the AI to keep it confidential
- D. Email the note to a coworker and let them paste it

**6. "Human-in-the-loop" means:**
- A. A person must type every word the AI writes
- B. The AI works alone and no one checks it
- C. The AI drafts, but a person reviews, corrects, and owns the final output
- D. You need at least two AI tools open at once

---

## Part B — Short answer (7–10)

**7.** In one sentence, explain the difference between *searching* (like a web search) and *generating*
(what a tool like a chat assistant does).

**8.** Rewrite this weak prompt into a stronger one using **Role · Task · Context · Format**:
> "Write something about our new delivery service."
(You may invent the details — pretend it is for your own job.)

**9.** Name the **three golden rules of responsible use** taught in this level (the short version is fine).

**10.** The AI gives you a confident-sounding statistic for a client email — for example, "On-time
delivery improved 27% last quarter." What should you do before sending it, and why?

---

## Answer key

> Self-check guide. Short-answer questions accept any reasonable wording — the ideas matter, not the
> exact words.

| # | Answer | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **B** | Generative AI *creates* new content; it is not just search. |
| 2 | **B** | It predicts likely wording from learned patterns, so it can be confidently wrong. |
| 3 | **B** | **Role · Task · Context · Format** — the prompt formula. |
| 4 | **C** | "Refine, don't restart" — keep the conversation and steer it. |
| 5 | **B** | Never paste sensitive data (PHI/PII) into public tools without an approved policy. |
| 6 | **C** | AI drafts; a person reviews and owns the result. |

**7.** A *search* finds and points you to information that already exists somewhere; *generating*
produces new wording (a draft, summary, or reply) built for your specific request. *Acceptable if the
learner captures "find existing" vs. "create new."*

**8.** A strong answer includes all four parts. Example:
```
Role: You are our logistics coordinator.
Task: Write a short announcement for our new same-day delivery service.
Context: For customers in the Miami area; we deliver within 4 hours for orders before 1 PM; friendly, professional tone.
Format: One paragraph (about 60 words) plus a one-line subject suggestion.
```
*Full credit if Role, Task, Context, and Format are all clearly present.*

**9.** Any wording of these three:
1. **Don't paste sensitive data** (PHI/PII/confidential) into public tools without an approved policy.
2. **Human-in-the-loop** — AI drafts, a person reviews and owns the output.
3. **Verify facts & figures** — AI can be confidently wrong (hallucinations).

**10.** **Verify the number before sending.** AI can produce a confident but invented figure (a
"hallucination"). Check it against your real records or source data, fix it if needed, and only then
send. *Full credit for "verify/check the figure" + "AI can be wrong / it may be made up."*
