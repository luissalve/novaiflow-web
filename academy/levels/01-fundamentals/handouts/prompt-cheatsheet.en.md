# Prompt Cheat-Sheet — One Page to Keep

Print this, pin it near your desk, and you have everything Level 1 taught in one glance. A good prompt is
just clear instructions to a very fast assistant. Use the formula, steal a ready-made prompt, and follow
the three golden rules.

---

## The formula: Role · Task · Context · Format

Fill these four parts and almost any request gets better. Think of it as briefing a new coworker.

| Part | Ask yourself | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Role** | Who should the AI act as? | "You are our front-desk coordinator." |
| **Task** | What exactly do you want? | "Write a reply to a late-delivery complaint." |
| **Context** | What does it need to know? | "Order was 2 days late; we offer a 10% credit; warm, professional tone." |
| **Format** | What shape should the answer take? | "A 4-sentence email plus a one-line subject." |

**Template you can copy:**
```
Role: You are {a role — e.g., our office manager}.
Task: {what you want done — one clear sentence}.
Context: {key facts, audience, tone, constraints}.
Format: {length, structure, language — e.g., a short email in Spanish}.
```

> **Refine, don't restart.** Your first answer is a draft. Instead of opening a new chat, reply in the
> same conversation: "Make it shorter," "More formal," "Add a deadline," "Now in Spanish." Steering beats
> starting over.

## 8 ready-to-use prompts

Copy one, swap the `{curly brace}` parts for your details, and go. These work for any front-line team.

**1 · Reply to a customer message**
```
Role: You are our customer-service rep. Task: Draft a reply to this message: "{paste the message}". Context: Tone is {friendly/formal}; our policy is {policy}. Format: A short email, under 120 words, with a subject line.
```

**2 · Summarize a long email or document**
```
Task: Summarize the text below for a busy manager. Context: They only need decisions and next steps. Format: 5 bullet points max, plus one line "Action needed:". Text: {paste here}.
```

**3 · Turn rough notes into a clean message**
```
Role: You are my writing assistant. Task: Turn these rough notes into a polished message. Context: Audience is {a client / my team}; keep my meaning, fix grammar. Format: One tidy paragraph. Notes: {paste notes}.
```

**4 · Write a job/announcement post**
```
Role: You are our recruiter. Task: Write a short post announcing {the role/news}. Context: Company is NovAIFlow-style SMB; tone upbeat but professional; key points: {list}. Format: 3 short paragraphs for social media.
```

**5 · Translate and adapt (EN ⇄ ES)**
```
Task: Translate the text below into {Spanish/English}. Context: Keep it natural for a {customer/parent/tenant}, not word-for-word; keep names and numbers exact. Format: Same structure as the original. Text: {paste here}.
```

**6 · Make a quick checklist or steps**
```
Task: Turn this goal into a simple checklist anyone on my team could follow: "{goal}". Context: We do this {weekly/per order}; flag any step where a person must double-check. Format: Numbered steps, max 8.
```

**7 · Brainstorm options fast**
```
Task: Give me 5 different options for {a subject line / a campaign name / a reply}. Context: For {audience}; tone {tone}; avoid {what to avoid}. Format: A numbered list, one line each.
```

**8 · Explain something in plain language**
```
Role: You are a patient teacher. Task: Explain "{topic/term}" so a non-expert understands it. Context: Reader is a {coworker / client} with no background. Format: 3 short sentences, no jargon, plus one everyday example.
```

## The 3 golden rules

Tape these where you can see them. They keep AI helpful *and* safe.

1. **Don't paste sensitive data.** Never put PHI, PII, or confidential info (names + health details,
   IDs, card numbers, private contracts) into a public tool without an approved policy. Remove or
   replace it first.
2. **Human-in-the-loop.** AI drafts; **you** review, fix, and own the result. Your name is on it, not
   the AI's.
3. **Verify facts & figures.** AI can sound confident and still be wrong ("hallucinations"). Check any
   number, name, date, or claim against your real records before you send it.
