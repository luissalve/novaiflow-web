# Glossary — AI Terms in Plain Language

The short, no-jargon dictionary for Level 1. Every term here came up in class or on your cheat-sheet.
Definitions are written for everyday business teams — if you only read one column, read "Plain meaning."

---

| Term | Plain meaning | Quick example |
|---|---|---|
| **Generative AI** | A tool that *creates* new content — text, summaries, images — based on your request, instead of just finding existing pages. | You ask for a customer reply and it writes one. |
| **AI model** (or "model") | The trained "engine" behind the tool. It learned patterns from huge amounts of text so it can produce useful answers. | Different models are like different brands of engine — some are faster, some more capable. |
| **Chat assistant** | The familiar chat window where you type a request and get an answer back, in a conversation. | The box where you type "Summarize this email." |
| **Prompt** | The instruction you give the AI — your request, in your own words. | "Write a 4-sentence thank-you note to a client." |
| **Prompt formula** | The Role · Task · Context · Format recipe for writing clear prompts. | See the cheat-sheet. |
| **Role** | Who you ask the AI to act as. | "You are our front-desk coordinator." |
| **Task** | What you want done, in one clear ask. | "Draft a late-delivery apology." |
| **Context** | The background facts the AI needs: audience, tone, constraints. | "Order was 2 days late; offer 10% credit." |
| **Format** | The shape of the answer you want. | "A short email with a subject line." |
| **Refine (iterate)** | Improving the answer by replying in the same chat instead of starting over. | "Make it shorter and more formal." |
| **Hallucination** | When AI states something false but sounds confident. Always verify facts and figures. | It invents a statistic that isn't real. |
| **Token** | The small chunks (roughly word-pieces) AI reads and writes in. You rarely deal with these directly. | Long documents = more tokens. |
| **Context window** | How much text the AI can "keep in mind" at once in a conversation. | Very long chats may "forget" the earliest details. |
| **Model limits / cutoff** | The model's knowledge has a date and gaps; it may not know recent or private events. | It won't know today's news or your internal numbers. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information — data that identifies a person (name, address, ID, card number). Don't paste into public tools without policy. | A customer's full name + account number. |
| **PHI** | Protected Health Information — health data tied to a person. Extra-sensitive; handle under approved policy only. | A patient's name + diagnosis. |
| **Confidential data** | Private business info: contracts, pricing, trade secrets, internal plans. Treat like PII. | A draft contract or salary list. |
| **Human-in-the-loop** | A person reviews, corrects, and owns the AI's output before it's used. | You edit the AI's draft and send it under your name. |
| **Responsible AI** | Using AI safely and ethically: protect data, verify facts, review output, disclose when required. | The three golden rules on your cheat-sheet. |
| **Public vs. approved tool** | A "public tool" is open on the internet; an "approved tool" is one your company cleared for sensitive work. | Use only approved tools for PHI/PII. |
| **Template** | A reusable, fill-in-the-blank prompt for a task you do often. | A saved "customer apology" prompt with `{blanks}`. |
| **Disclosure** | Telling people, when required, that AI helped produce something. | Noting AI assistance where your industry expects it. |
