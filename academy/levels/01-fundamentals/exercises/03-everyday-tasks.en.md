# Exercise 3 — Three Everyday Tasks

You now know the **Role · Task · Context · Format** scaffold and how to refine. Time to put it to work
across your real week. In this exercise you'll pick **three different everyday tasks** and write a
solid prompt for each. These three become your **Level 1 deliverable**: a small starter kit of prompts
for your role that you can use immediately.

Variety is the point — choose tasks that are genuinely different (a writing one, a summarizing one, a
quick-answer one), so you see how flexible the same scaffold is.

> **Time:** about 20 minutes · **You need:** any chat AI assistant and three real tasks from your job.

---

## The scenario

Every role has small, repeating tasks that eat up time. Here are common ones — pick three that fit
**your** week (mix categories on purpose):

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Write** something | A client email, a reminder, a social post, a product description |
| **Summarize** something | A long email thread, a 2-page document, meeting notes into action items |
| **Reply** to someone | A customer question, a review, a complaint, a vendor request |
| **Brainstorm / plan** | Ideas for a promotion, an agenda for a meeting, a checklist for a task |
| **Translate / adjust tone** | Turn a Spanish note into English, make a message more formal or warmer |

---

## Your task

Write **three prompts**, one per task, each using the full scaffold. Use the template below three
times. Keep the tasks different from each other.

### Template (use 3×)

```text
--- PROMPT {1 / 2 / 3} ---
Role: Act as a {role that fits this task}.
Task: {the one clear thing you want done}.
Context: {the facts it needs — audience, situation, key details, tone; NO sensitive data}.
Format: {length, structure, and language — e.g. bullet list, under 80 words, in Spanish}.
```

### Worked examples (don't copy — adapt to your job)

```text
--- PROMPT 1 (Write) ---
Role: Act as a friendly retail associate at a small home-goods store.
Task: Write a short product description for a ceramic coffee mug.
Context: It's hand-glazed, holds 12 oz, dishwasher-safe, sells for $18. Our customers like cozy,
homey language. Avoid hype words like "amazing."
Format: 2 short sentences, warm tone, in English.
```

```text
--- PROMPT 2 (Summarize) ---
Role: Act as an office assistant at a law firm.
Task: Summarize the email below into 4 plain-language bullet points a busy attorney can scan.
Context: The email is a scheduling back-and-forth about a deposition. I'll paste it after this.
Keep names but remove pleasantries. (Note: only paste documents allowed by firm policy.)
Format: 4 bullets, each under 15 words, in English.
```

```text
--- PROMPT 3 (Reply) ---
Role: Act as a hotel guest-services agent.
Task: Draft a polite reply to a guest's online review.
Context: The guest gave 4 stars, loved the staff, but said the room was a bit noisy. We want to
thank them, acknowledge the noise, and invite them back. Stay gracious, never defensive.
Format: One short paragraph, under 90 words, warm and professional, in English.
```

---

## Steps

1. Choose **three different** tasks from your real week (use at least two different categories).
2. Write a full prompt for each using the template.
3. Run all three. Read each answer — would you use it after a quick edit?
4. Refine any that miss (add a missing detail, tighten the format) — just like Exercise 2.
5. Save the three finished prompts together. **This is your Level 1 deliverable.**

---

## Success looks like

- [ ] You have **three** prompts, each with **Role · Task · Context · Format**.
- [ ] The three tasks are genuinely **different** (not three versions of the same email).
- [ ] Each answer is usable **after a quick human review** and matches the format you asked for.
- [ ] None of the prompts contains sensitive data (patient info, account numbers, confidential text).
- [ ] You saved all three together as a reusable starter kit for your role.

> **Remember (Responsible AI):** these prompts are for *everyday, non-sensitive* tasks. When a task
> involves personal or confidential information, follow your company's approved policy first — and a
> person always reviews and owns the final result before it's sent or shared.
