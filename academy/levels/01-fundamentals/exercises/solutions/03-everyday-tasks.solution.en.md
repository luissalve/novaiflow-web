# Solution — Exercise 3: Three Everyday Tasks

These are **model prompts** for three genuinely different everyday tasks. Your learners' tasks will
differ — that's expected and good. Use these to check that each prompt has all four scaffold parts and
that the three tasks span different *categories*.

> **For facilitators:** the deliverable is three saved prompts. Quality bar: each one names Role, Task,
> Context, Format, contains no sensitive data, and produces a usable draft.

---

## Model prompt 1 — Write (retail product description)

```text
Role: Act as a friendly retail associate at a small home-goods store.
Task: Write a short product description for a ceramic coffee mug.
Context: Hand-glazed, holds 12 oz, dishwasher-safe, sells for $18. Our customers like cozy, homey
language. Avoid hype words like "amazing."
Format: 2 short sentences, warm tone, in English.
```

**Likely result:**

```text
Start your morning the cozy way with our hand-glazed 12 oz ceramic mug — made to keep your coffee
warm and your hands happy. Dishwasher-safe and built for everyday use, it's a simple comfort at $18.
```

---

## Model prompt 2 — Summarize (law firm email → bullets)

```text
Role: Act as an office assistant at a law firm.
Task: Summarize the email below into 4 plain-language bullet points an attorney can scan.
Context: The email is a scheduling back-and-forth about a deposition. Keep names, drop pleasantries.
(Only paste documents your firm policy allows.) [paste email here]
Format: 4 bullets, each under 15 words, in English.
```

**Likely result:**

```text
- Opposing counsel proposes the deposition for June 18 at 10am.
- They request our witness list by June 10.
- Location: their downtown office; remote option available on request.
- They ask us to confirm by Friday.
```

---

## Model prompt 3 — Reply (hotel review response)

```text
Role: Act as a hotel guest-services agent.
Task: Draft a polite reply to a guest's online review.
Context: 4-star review; guest loved the staff but said the room was a bit noisy. Thank them,
acknowledge the noise, invite them back. Stay gracious, never defensive.
Format: One short paragraph, under 90 words, warm and professional, in English.
```

**Likely result:**

```text
Thank you so much for your kind words about our team — they truly make our hotel feel like home. We're
sorry the room noise took the shine off an otherwise lovely stay; we're always working to keep things
peaceful and your note helps us do better. We'd love the chance to welcome you back and show you an
even quieter, more restful visit. Safe travels, and we hope to see you again soon.
```

---

## Why these three make a good starter kit

| # | Category | What the scaffold did |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Write** | Role set a cozy voice; Format capped it at 2 sentences and banned hype. |
| 2 | **Summarize** | Task + Format turned a messy thread into 4 scannable bullets; Context kept names, dropped fluff. |
| 3 | **Reply** | Context steered tone (gracious, not defensive); Format kept it short and warm. |

The same four-part scaffold handled three very different jobs. That's the takeaway: **one method,
endless tasks.**

---

## Common mistakes and quick fixes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| All three tasks are basically the same email | Pick from different categories (write / summarize / reply). |
| Forgot the Format on the summary | Add "4 bullets, under 15 words each" so it's scannable. |
| Pasted a real client's confidential email | Only paste what policy allows; redact names/numbers if unsure. |
| Output too long | Always set a word or sentence cap in Format. |

> **Remember (Responsible AI):** these are everyday, non-sensitive tasks. For anything personal or
> confidential, follow your company's policy and have a person review and own the result before it
> ships.
