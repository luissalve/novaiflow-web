# Exercise 1 — Your First Useful Prompt

Welcome to your first hands-on exercise. The goal is simple: write **one prompt** that gets an AI
assistant to help you with a *real* task from your workday — something you would otherwise do by hand.
No special words, no code, no tricks. Just a clear request, written the way you would brief a helpful
new coworker.

By the end you will have a prompt you can actually reuse on Monday morning.

> **Time:** about 15 minutes · **You need:** any chat AI assistant (the one used in class) and a real
> task in mind.

---

## The scenario

Think of a small writing or thinking task you do often and would happily hand off. Examples by role:

| Your world | A real task you could hand off |
|---|---|
| Logistics / freight | Draft a short status email to a client about a delayed shipment |
| Clinic (front desk) | Write a friendly appointment-reminder message |
| Law firm | Summarize a 2-page letter into 5 plain-language bullet points |
| School | Draft a note to parents about a schedule change |
| Real estate | Write a listing description for a 3-bed, 2-bath home |
| Retail | Reply politely to a customer asking about a refund |
| Hotel | Respond to a guest's online review |

Pick **one**. It must be a task with no sensitive data in it for now (we cover that in Exercise 4).

---

## Your task

Write a single prompt that asks the assistant to do that task for you. To make it clear and useful,
fill in the **Role · Task · Context · Format** scaffold below. This is the backbone of every good
prompt you will ever write.

### The Role · Task · Context · Format scaffold

| Part | What it means | Ask yourself |
|---|---|---|
| **Role** | Who the AI should act as | "Act as a friendly customer-service rep / office assistant / etc." |
| **Task** | The one thing you want done | "Write…", "Summarize…", "Draft…", "List…" |
| **Context** | The facts it needs to do it well | Names, dates, the situation, your audience, your tone |
| **Format** | What the answer should look like | Length, bullets vs. paragraph, email vs. list, language (EN/ES) |

Fill in this template, then paste the assembled prompt into the assistant:

```text
Role: Act as a {role, e.g. helpful front-desk assistant at a small clinic}.
Task: {the one thing you want, e.g. write a short appointment-reminder message}.
Context: {the facts it needs — who it's for, the situation, dates, the tone you want}.
Format: {how the answer should look — e.g. 3 short sentences, friendly, in English}.
```

### Worked starting point (don't copy — adapt)

```text
Role: Act as a helpful logistics coordinator at a freight company.
Task: Write a short, calm email to a client letting them know their shipment is delayed.
Context: The shipment is 2 days late due to a port backlog. The client is a regular and values
quick, honest updates. We expect it to arrive Thursday. Keep the tone professional and reassuring.
Format: A short email, under 120 words, with a subject line, in English.
```

---

## Steps

1. Pick your one real task from the table above (or your own).
2. Fill in **Role · Task · Context · Format** in the template.
3. Paste the full prompt into the assistant and send it.
4. Read the result out loud. Would you actually send it? If not, jot down what's off.
5. Save the prompt somewhere (a notes file) — this is the start of your personal prompt library.

---

## Success looks like

Tick each box. A strong first prompt will hit all five.

- [ ] The prompt names a **Role**, a **Task**, the **Context**, and a **Format**.
- [ ] A coworker reading only your prompt would understand exactly what you want.
- [ ] The assistant's answer is on-topic and roughly the length/shape you asked for.
- [ ] You would be comfortable using the result **after a quick human review** (you, the person, own it).
- [ ] No sensitive data (names of patients, account numbers, confidential details) was included.

> **Remember (Responsible AI):** the assistant *drafts*; **you review and own** the final wording.
> Always read before you send, and never paste confidential or personal data into a public tool
> without your company's approved policy.
